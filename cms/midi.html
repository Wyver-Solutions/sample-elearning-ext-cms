MIDI

MIDI (short for Musical Instrument Digital Interface) is a technical standard that describes a communications protocol, digital interface, and electrical connectors that connect a wide variety of electronic musical instruments, computers, and related audio devices.[1] A single MIDI link can carry up to sixteen channels of information, each of which can be routed to a separate device.

MIDI carries event messages that specify notation, pitch, velocity, vibrato, panning, and clock signals (which set tempo). For example, a MIDI keyboard or other controller might trigger a sound module to generate sound produced by a keyboard amplifier. MIDI data can be transferred via midi cable, or recorded to a sequencer to be edited or played back.
